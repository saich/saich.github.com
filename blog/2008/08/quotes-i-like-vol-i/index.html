Quotes I Like :: Vol. I

19 August, 2008

  • Expect the best. Prepare for the worst. Capitalize on what comes. - Z Ziglar
  • In great attempts it is glorius even to fail. - Cassius Longinus
  • The trouble with our times is that the future is not what it used to be. - Paul Valery
  • Trying to forget someone you love is trying to remember someone you never met. - Angelina Jolie
  • Experience is not what happens to a man. It is what a man does with what happens to him. - Aldous Huxley
  • I don’t pray for a lighter load, but for a stronger back. - Brookes
  • Time ripens all things; no man is born wise. - Miguel de Cervante
  • A great civilization is not conquered from without until it has destroyed itself from within. - W.Durant
  • The best way out of a difficulty is through it. - Robert Frost
  • Every accomplishment begins with a decision to try. - Edward Kelly
  • Happiness is nothing more than good health & a bad memory. - Albert Schweitzer
  • Most people fail in life because they major in minor things. - Anthony Robbins
  • Pick battles big enough to matter, small enough to win. - J.Kozol
  • If you aspire to the highest place, it is no disgrace to reach at the second or even the third place. - Cicero
  • People generally quarrel because they cannot argue. - G.K.Chesterton
  • Millions said the apple fell but Newton was the one to ask why. - Bernard M.Baruch
  • Bring ideas in and entertain them royally, for one of them may be the king. - Mark Van Doren
  • The powers of mind are like rays of the sun dissipated. When they are concentrated, they illumine. - Swami Vivekananda
  • Always behave like a duck - Keep calm and unruffled on the surface but paddle with great vigor underneath. - Jacob Braude
  • The aim of argument, or of discussion, should not be victory, but progress. - Joseph Joubert
  • At times, it is better to keep your mouth shut and let people wonder if you’re fool than to open it and remove all their doubts. - James Sinclair
  • A man may die, nations may rise and fall, but an idea lives on. Ideas have endurance without death. - John F.Kennedy
  • Always forgive your enemies - Nothing annoys them so much. - Oscar Wilde
  • Success is that old ABC - ability, breaks and courage. - Charles Luckman
  • If you would not like to be forgotten as soon as you are dead, either write things worth reading or do things worth writing. - Benjamin Franklin
  • A Ship is always safe at the shore - but that is NOT what it is built for. - Albert Einstein
